Fixed scope, fixed date projects and effects on slack

31.07.2015 08:36 Uncategorized

datoon83 - Oct 3, 2015

I’ve often thought the same, my issue is this though. I don’t really believe that many companies know what the value of the features they are delivering - having no deadline means that there is no urgency to deliver anything. While I hate delivery dates I understand that most companies aren’t mature enough to use things like Cost of Delay. What is this feature worth and how much are we loosing by not delivering it today. Dates are an unfortunate necessary evil in this case - they focus the mind on the delivery of a set of features - sometimes forcing innovation. The thing I’d argue for with date delivery driven organisation is that scope is variable - i.e. give us a set of features in priority order, and understand that the bottom features may not get done. While I agree with slack, I know that with a date driven organisation this can be created and managed (I’ve done it!). It takes some creative thinking and knowledge of systems that you create!
