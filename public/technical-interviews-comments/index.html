Technical Interviews

24.10.2016 18:40 Uncategorized

Paul D’Ambra - Oct 1, 2016

I’ve found having people code review something like this https://gist.github.com/pauldambra/f5da226d4e51bc95ef11 ahead of time was a good way to get a window into how they think. You can talk about what they choose to mention; why they choose it; and the same for things they don’t choose. Or how they’d address it, what to focus on first in coaching, or fixing it. Obviously you could tweak it so the issues are more subtle or have examples from the code they’ll actually be working with. I suppose you could do a similar thing with how to architect a system or approach a legacy project. It worked well with people with a long history or with personal recommendations where there isn’t as much value on watching them write software in the artificial interview environment
